ANSWER: Ack, no, please don't take that message way from the column!!

A couple of things to keep in mind. First, the obvious, but reminders never hurt: People have their own developmental pace.

That means some 15-year-olds are thinking college and do have a career goal taking shape. And good for them! That doesn't mean there's something wrong with other kids that age who haven't progressed to that point. (I didn't know what I wanted to do with my life till I was 30.)

ANSWER: I am going to refer you to a licensed health professional, which I am not. The caution not to sleep beyond a typical night's sleep is passed along from best practices I discussed with a doctor recently, in the specific context of things people with depression can do outside of medical treatment to ease their depression symptoms and boost the effectiveness of their treatment.

So -- medical treatment would be things like medication and therapy.

These other things included regular exercise; social interaction; attention to nutrition; regular, short (ie not overwhelming), structured attention to keeping spaces clean and uncluttered (e.g. 2 x 15 minutes a day on clearing workspace, doing laundry, basic management); connection with purposeful activities. That last one is a broad category, but covers hobbies, causes, arts, music, etc. Among these was a regular sleep schedule; sleeping in and napping were considered disruptive to the overnight sleep schedule, which contributed to nighttime restlessness and anxiety, daytime grogginess, sense of sluggishness and futility.

Again -- your care plan is personal and that's why the medical consult is your advice.

I spelled out my backing info for context. I also want to say these non-medical things are not depression cures or treatments -- there's often a conflation problem when careful life management is helpful. If anyone tries to use any of what I'm saying here as a justification not to treat depression as an illness or not to go to a doctor, or to yell at me for implying that people can exercise their depression away, or that they can't! then allow me to state here these are all willful misreadings of my point.
