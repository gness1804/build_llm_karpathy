ANSWER: I can see why you want the therapist off the scene. Your husband's "first instincts" response sounded much more in your favor -- mainly because it let you believe this would all go away and you could go back to the marriage you thought you had before your awful discovery.

avatar
Carolyn Hax
Advice Columnist
ANSWER: Hard to answer, because such a line is personal, of course.

But i think there is a fair way to look at it generally that takes into account different personalities.

Being human has two types of "work." There's the work of life management, such as chores and a job and keeping cars safe and registered. Then there's the work of self-management, which includes hygiene, emotional regulation, social awareness. Oversimplified, but I'm too slow as it is.

When you're with someone, you need to manage your workload in both. Hold up your share of the chores and other life management, and not let the self-management slide.

Good so far.

To my mind, a couple isn't working when being in it demands too much work in either category. Now, their life work might just be crazy -- young kids, etc. -- and as long as they're sharing it and both equally flat out exhausted with "too much" work, then okay. That's temporary and they can get through it, and true sharing means they're a good pair. It's lopsidedness that will doom them -- when one is doing everything and the other says "You wanted kids I didn't!" or whatever.

That is also true of self-management work. If you're kind of a mess and your partner asks you to get some help, and you're in therapy and it's hard, but you're learning healthier ways, then your self-management work will go up for a bit but ultimately go down as you get healthier. That's a good thing.

If instead you're being asked by your partner to, basically, suppress yourself into a little box (because you're "loud" or whatever), then you're going to work yourself to exhaustion on the self-management side, trying to be someone you're not.

That, to me, is the line: when being with someone doesn't make your life better, but instead drains or exhausts you (in a chore sense or self-management sense), with no prospect of improvement.

Hope that helps.
