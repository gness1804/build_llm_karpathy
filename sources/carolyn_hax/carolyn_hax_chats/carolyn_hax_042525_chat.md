avatar
Carolyn Hax
Advice Columnist
ANSWER: The way to reconcile with firm boundaries is to understand what you do and don't control, and then be very clear in your purpose for and management of the part you control.

Let's look at your list of wants and don't wants:

1. "I want my sister to acknowledge the harm her behavior has caused." That is not something you control. You control whether you ask for it, sure, but it's up to your sister whether you get it. So I caution against this, because it puts a big part of your path forward in your sister's hands.

2. "I don't want to get sucked in to an unproductive fight again." This is something you control. When you feel a fight coming on or hitting an unproductive point, then you can walk away from it. Entirely up to you.

3. "I would like to be on better terms, but I don't want to be an enabler either." This is something you control. You can make an overture to your sister to resume the relationship, carefully. You can decide beforehand what behaviors you will not enable, accept or encourage. What that means in practice is that when you are, say, subjected to abusive behavior like getting screamed at, you will not listen to it or scream back or apologize for whatever set it off. You will simply end the conversation and step away. Knowing it is a health issue, you may choose to call again at a pre-determined interval, and continue this arm's length engagement. Or you may choose to check in less often or not at all.

As for your family, you let them know you are engaging on your terms and are not seeking input on this. If they start to intrude.
